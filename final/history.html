History of Tesla

Founding of Tesla

Tesla was founded in 2003 by engineers who decided to show that electric cars could be better than gasoline cars in performance, security, and aesthetics. The first pair was Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning while the next trio was Elon Musk, JB Straubel, and Ian Wright. Ever since, Tesla's main ambition has been to accelerate the transformation of the world with energy that is clean.

First Breakthrough

In 2008, Tesla manufactured its first vehicle, the Tesla Roadster. It was one of the first electric vehicles capable of more than 200 miles on a single charge proving that electric vehicles can be in competition with normal cars in range and performance.

Model S and Model X

After this, Tesla expanded with 2 new models. Model S which is a full size sedan introduced in 2012 making Tesla aslo a manufacturer of premium electric cars. In 2015, they introduced Model X which is am SUV which offers more space and practicality while still maintaining performance and sustainability.

Model 3 and Model Y

Tesla wanted to make theirs cars more accessible. So in 2017, Model 3 was introduced to be more affordable. Then in 2020, Tesla released Model Y, a compact electric SUV designed to appeal to a broader market. Both of these models expanded Tesla's global reach and helped popularize electric vehicles.

Expanding Beyond Vehicles

After a while, Tesla expanded into manufacturing clean energy and sustainable technology. Tesla now also offers energy storage, battery technology and related solutions.
